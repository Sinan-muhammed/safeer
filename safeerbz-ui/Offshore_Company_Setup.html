Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about offshore company setup

What happens if I don't pay the annual renewal fees?

Late payment of annual renewal fees can cause extra fines. Your company will lose its good operating status and may risk being struck off. We recommend setting up timely reminders or allowing us to manage renewals on your behalf.

Can the same person be a shareholder of the company and act as its director at the same time?

Yes, in most offshore jurisdictions, a person can simultaneously be a shareholder and director of a company. This is common in small business structures. However, specific requirements vary by jurisdiction. Contact us for jurisdiction-specific guidance.

What types of activity/industry require a specific business license?

Regulated industries such as financial services, insurance, real estate, healthcare, and telecommunications typically require specific business licenses. We'll guide you through the licensing requirements for your specific industry.

What types of business should be an offshore company?

Offshore companies are ideal for businesses involved in international trade, import/export, consulting services, digital services, investment holdings, and companies seeking tax optimization. If your business has international operations or clients, an offshore structure may benefit you.

What is the difference between the Standard and Premium package?

Our Standard package includes basic company formation and registration. The Premium package adds comprehensive support including bank account opening assistance, compliance management, and ongoing advisory services. Visit our Packages page for detailed comparisons.

How long does the offshore company formation process take?

The timeline varies depending on the jurisdiction and specific requirements. Typically, the process takes 2-4 weeks from submission to completion. We expedite timelines where possible and keep you informed at every step.
